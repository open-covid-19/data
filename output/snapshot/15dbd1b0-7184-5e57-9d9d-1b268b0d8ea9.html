Notes
  1. ^ Since 5 March, the figures in this column also include a Northern Territory case diagnosed on 4 March, which has been counted as a NSW case by NSW Health from 5 March after the case returned to his home state.[4]
  2. ^ On 27 March, NT Health added a case of a Queenslander diagnosed in Darwin yesterday to its tally, adjusting the total number of cases in the NT from 12 on 26 March to 13.[13][14] On 27 March, NT Health reported a Territorian being treated by NSW Health, but it did not count the case in its tally.[14] From 27 March onwards, the NT tally in this column includes the Territorian treated by NSW Health but not the Queenslander diagnosed in Darwin, so the total number of cases in the NT recorded in this column will be the same as that reported by NT Health.
  3. ^ Refers to the net difference in the number of cases reported.
  4. ^ The figures for 4 March include 10 cases from Diamond Princess. The number repatriated from the ship are included in the state totals as follows: Qld (3), Vic (4), SA (1), WA (2, one of whom died on 1 March).[15][1]
  5. ^ The figures for 22 March include 26 from the cruise ship Ruby Princess:[16] NSW 18, WA 6, other 2. The number climbed to 133 on 24 March: 107 in NSW, 26 interstate.[17] A passenger from the ship died on 24 March in Sydney. Since then, more than 440 cases have been traced to the ship, including more than a dozen deaths.[18][19]
  6. ^ Numbers from 5 April are based on the standardised Australian Government Infographic which is reported daily at 15:00 AEST. Therefore, there may be some discrepancies between the State's reporting and the Australian Government's, such as for Tasmania, who usually update their numbers after 15:00 AEST.
  7. ^ The number of cases in Tasmania decreased from 82 on 4 April to 81, due to differences between State and Federal Government reporting. As of 5 April, all Tasmanian values are based on the Australian Government's Infographic
  8. ^ The number of cases from 11 April in NSW was revised down to 2,847. Nine patients tested in NSW have been identified as residents of ACT and Queensland and living in their home jurisdiction, while another previously reported case was a false positive. 7 new cases were identified on 12 April.[27]
  9. ^ There were no new cases on 12 April in the ACT. The number of cases in the ACT reduced from 103 to 102 due to a confirmed case determined not to have COVID-19 after further investigation and testing.[28]
  10. ^ There were five new cases on 15 April for Queensland, however the total cases from the previous day was revised down to 994 from 998
  11. ^ There were five new cases on 16 April for Queensland, however the total cases from the previous day was revised down to 996 from 999
  12. ^ There were five new cases in New South Wales on the 22nd of April, however three cases previously reported in the NSW totals have now been excluded after further testing, reflecting an adjusted overall total of 2,971 confirmed cases.
  13. ^ There were two new cases in Victoria on the 22nd of April, however two interstate cases were removed and added to their respective state's total, meaning that there was no change in the overall number of cases in Victoria.
  14. ^ There were seven new cases in New South Wales on the 24th of April, however one case previously reported in the NSW totals was excluded after further testing, reflecting an adjusted overall total of 2,982 confirmed cases.
  15. ^ There were two new cases in Queensland on the 24th of April, however two cases previously reported in the Queensland totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting no net change in the number of cases.
  16. ^ There were two new cases in Queensland on the 25th of April, however two cases previously reported in the Queensland totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting no net change in the number of cases.
  17. ^ There was one new case on the 27th of April in Victoria, however one of Victoria's previously claimed cases was moved to the NSW tally, hence reflecting no net change
  18. ^ There were eleven new cases in New South Wales on the 29th of April, however four cases previously reported in the New South Wales totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting a net increase of 7 cases.
  19. ^ There were two new cases in New South Wales on the 30th of April, however two cases previously reported in the New South Wales totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting no net increase in number of cases.
  20. ^ There were no new cases in Queensland on the 30th of April. Once case previously reported in the Queensland totals was excluded after being revised, reflecting a net decrease in number of cases.
  21. ^ There was one new case in New South Wales on the 4th of May, however three cases previously reported in the New South Wales totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting a net decrease of 2 in the number of cases.
  22. ^ There were three new cases in New South Wales on the 5th of May, however one case previously reported in the New South Wales totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting a net increase of 2 in the number of cases.
  23. ^ There was nine new cases in New South Wales on the 6th of May, however two cases previously reported in the New South Wales totals were excluded after being revised, reflecting a net increase of 7 in the number of cases.
  24. ^ There were two new cases in Queensland on the 10th of May, however two cases previously reported were excluded after being revised, reflecting no net change in the number of cases.
  25. ^ There was one new case in New South Wales on the 11th of May, however one case previously reported was excluded after being revised, reflecting no net change in the number of cases.
  26. ^ There were seventeen new cases in Victoria on the 12th of May, however two cases previously reported were excluded after being revised and four cases were moved to the Queensland tally.
  27. ^ There were no new cases in Queensland on the 12th of May, four cases previously reported in Victoria and two cases previously reported in Western Australia were moved to the Queensland tally.
  28. ^ There was one new case in Western Australia on the 12th of May, however two cases previously reported were moved to the Queensland tally.
  29. ^ There were no new cases in Western Australia on May 18, three historical cases were identified through serology testing.
  30. ^ There were four new cases in New South Wales on the 20th of May, however one case previously reported was excluded after being revised, reflecting a net increase of three cases.
  31. ^ There were four new cases in Victoria on the 21st of May, however two cases previously reported was excluded due to duplication and one case was transferred interstate, reflecting a net increase of one case.
